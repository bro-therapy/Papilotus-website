## What makes a clip interact-worthy (every 2026 finding I could find)

These aren't opinions — they're statistical findings from 2026 platform studies. Build every clip to satisfy as many as possible.

### The first second decides everything
**Hooks in the first second = 41% higher retention** ([OpusClip, 2026](https://www.opus.pro/research/best-video-hooks-instagram)). On TikTok, the algorithm shows your video to 200-500 random users in the first hour; their completion rate decides whether you get pushed further.

**Practical:** Pick `start` timestamps where something *visibly happens* on frame one. Not a fade-up. Not silence. The lyric should already be visible by the time the first beat lands. If your clip starts on a still face, scrap it and pick a different second.

### Subtitle the lyric. Always.
**Burned-in text = +24% shares, +29% reach. Silent video = -19% engagement.** ([Marketing LTB stats, 2026](https://marketingltb.com/blog/statistics/tiktok-videos-statistics/))

The factory does this automatically. Even if your viewer's sound is off (60%+ of mobile viewers in 2026), the lyric still hits.

### Face-centered clips outperform by 35%
On the hook clips and editorial portraits, **face-on-camera beats environmental shots** for engagement. People stop scrolling for faces. Your brand voice keeps the face "tired, distant, slight smirk" — that fits perfectly. You don't need to smile or perform; just be on-screen.

**Practical:** Days where the calendar says "AI Portrait" or "Editorial" — lean into face-forward framing. Days where it says "Hook Loop" — alternate face-visible with environment-only across the variants.

### POV format outperforms by 17%
The calendar uses "POV:" captions extensively. Research confirms: **POV-framed videos do 17% better than standard format.** The "POV: you miss the chaos, not the person" structure works because it triggers the viewer's own memory.

**Why it works for dark R&B specifically:** The whole genre is about late-night specific feelings. "POV: you ruined the only good thing" reads as the viewer's thought, not yours. They project themselves into the song.

### Comment-bait captions = 2.4x more comments
**"Which scene was hardest?" / "Red room or rainy highway?" / "Send this to nobody"** — these aren't generic. They're algorithm fuel. The TikTok algorithm reads comment volume in the first hour as a signal to push wider.

**Practical:** The calendar marks specific posts for this (Days 18, 22, 33, 51, 56). Don't water it down with "let me know what you think!" — be specific. "Which line ruined you?" Not "thoughts?"

### DM shares are weighted 3-5x higher than likes (IG specifically)
**"Send this to nobody" / "Save this for the drive home"** — these are designed to trigger people sending the video to one specific person in their life. That's an algorithmic gold mine on IG.

**Practical:** Captions like "send this to nobody" outperform "share if you relate." Specificity > generality. The brand voice already has this — keep using it.

### Save rate matters more than like rate
**Save rate ≥ 2% = exceptional** ([FYP Now, Feb 2026](https://fypnow.com/blog/tiktok-engagement-rate-calculator-guide)). Saves tell the algorithm the content has *lasting value*. Lyric posts and POV emotional edits get saved at the highest rates.

**Practical:** Black-background lyric stills (Days 4, 37, 55) are pure save-bait. They look like screenshots people will use as wallpaper or notes-app references. Pin one in your highlights.

### Repeat the hook. Don't rewrite it.
**"Most successful artists post DOZENS of snippets before a song goes viral. Different angles, different lyrics, different vibes."** ([Vidlo, 2026 music strategy](https://vidlo.video/blog/tiktok-strategy-for-musicians/))

This is why the calendar uses ONE chorus across 14 days, then tests verse/bridge/outro lyrics in week 5. Audience familiarity = saves. The 30th time a stranger hears your hook, they save it. Not the first.

### Original Audio matters on TikTok
When you post your song clip on TikTok, mark it as **Original Audio**. If other users start using it for their own videos, your sound page becomes a discovery engine. This is how unknown songs blow up.

**Practical:** Every TikTok hook post should have the song as the audio source. Not trending sounds, not background music — your own audio. The factory keeps audio intact.

## Length cheatsheet

| Clip type | Length | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Hook loops | 5-8 sec | Long enough to feel the song, short enough to loop endlessly. TikTok algorithm rewards 100%+ completion. |
| POV emotional edits | 7-12 sec | Need room for caption + lyric + emotional turn. |
| Lyric stills (Reels/Feed) | 4-7 sec | Single line, single visual. Anything longer = scroll. |
| Final teasers | 6 sec | Max impact, no time to lose attention. |
| Main teaser (Day 15, 23) | 12 sec | Allows date card at the end. |
| Extended visualizers | 25-30 sec | For lyric/mood posts where vibe is the product. |
| Behind-the-scenes | 6-10 sec | Authentic > polished. Phone screenshots, studio shots. |

## What to AVOID cutting

- **Anything starting on a black frame or fade** — first second is wasted
- **Clips where your face is in profile or fully obscured** — face-front wins
- **Clips longer than 12 sec for hook content** — completion rate plummets
- **Performance shots where you're singing the wrong syllable on screen** — the lyric overlay has to sync with what your mouth is doing
- **Anything that needs context to make sense** — every clip should work as a standalone

## A workflow rhythm that doesn't burn you out

Once per week, **2-3 hour session**:

1. **Open your music video** in a video player. Note timestamps of 5-6 strong visual moments you haven't used yet. Add them as new rows in `02_clips.csv`.
2. **Run the factory** (`python3 clip_factory.py`). 5 minutes.
3. **Take the 9:16 outputs to Higgsfield.** Generate 3-4 background variations of each (motel, rain, red room, chrome). 30-60 minutes.
4. **Drop the AI variations back into `01_input/`**, add new CSV rows pointing to them with the lyric you want this week.
5. **Run the factory again.** 5 minutes.
6. **Drop final outputs into Postiz** using the Day numbers in filenames. 20 minutes.

That's it. The week is scheduled. Repeat next Sunday.
